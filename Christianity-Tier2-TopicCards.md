- **Oldest complete New Testament:** Codex Sinaiticus - Dated ~330-360 CE (300+ years after Jesus), written in Greek
- **Language barrier:** Jesus spoke Aramaic, but NO Aramaic originals exist. All manuscripts are Greek translations

## üìå Their Core Claim
> "Jesus is God incarnate, the second person of the Holy Trinity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit)."

- Rules of logic: Something cannot be its opposite

  - Up is not down
  - Eternal is not temporal
  - All-powerful is not weak
  - All-knowing is not ignorant
- **God cannot do things that contradict His attributes**
  - God cannot do evil (He is perfectly good)
  - God cannot be ignorant (He is all-knowing)
  - God cannot be weak (He is all-powerful)
- If Jesus was hungry, tired, ignorant, and tempted - he had HUMAN attributes, not divine attributes
- Therefore: Jesus cannot be God

### 2. **Jesus Denied Being God**

### 3. **Trinity Was Invented at Church Councils (325-451 CE)**
- **Word "Trinity" NEVER appears in the Bible**
- **Council of Nicea (325 CE):** Emperor Constantine (not bishops) proposed it, threatened exile
- **Council of Constantinople (381 CE):** Holy Spirit as 3rd person established - 300+ years after Jesus!
- **Council of Chalcedon (451 CE):** Hypostatic Union finalized - 400+ years after Jesus
- <span style="color:blue;">**Saint Jerome:**</span> World "awoke with a groan to find itself Arian" (359 CE) - non-Trinitarians were majority

### 4. **The ONLY Trinity Verse Was FORGED**
- **1 John 5:7** (only explicit Trinity statement): Not in any Greek manuscript before 15th century
- Erasmus was pressured to include it - someone manufactured a Greek manuscript!
- Removed from modern translations (RSV, NIV, ESV)

### 5. **Jesus Had Human Limitations (NOT God's Attributes)**
- Hungry, thirsty, sleepy, tired (Matthew 4:2, John 19:28, Luke 8:23, John 4:6)
- Ignorant: Didn't know the Hour (Mark 13:32), didn't know fig season (Mark 11:13)
- Tempted by Satan 40 days (Mark 1:13) - but "God cannot be tempted" (James 1:13)
- Fled, hid from danger (John 7:1, John 10:39, John 11:54)
- Arrested, beaten, condemned (John 18:12, Luke 22:63, Mark 14:64)

### 6. **Leading Christian Scholars Admit Jesus Never Claimed Divinity**
- <span style="color:blue;">**1984 Survey:**</span> Only 11 of 31 Anglican bishops insisted Jesus was God
- <span style="color:blue;">**Archbishop Michael Ramsey:**</span> "Jesus did not claim deity for himself"

# TOPIC 3: Crucifixion & Atonement

## üìå Their Core Claim
> "Jesus died on the cross as a sacrifice for humanity's sins. His death is necessary for salvation."

## ‚ö° Your Main Counter-Points

### 1. **Logical Inconsistency: All-Just God Cannot Forgive Through Sacrifice of Another**
- **Why would an all-just God forgive mankind by sacrificing someone else?**
  - In any human court, punishing an innocent person for the guilty is the definition of INJUSTICE
  - How can God's justice be satisfied by the most unjust act - punishing the innocent for the guilty?
- **Why would God NEED a sacrifice to forgive sins?**
  - Is God unable to forgive directly? That would limit His power
  - Is God unwilling to forgive without blood? That would limit His mercy
  - If God can forgive, why doesn't He just forgive?
- **Vicarious atonement contradicts justice:**
  - Justice means each person receives what they deserve
  - Transferring punishment to an innocent person is the OPPOSITE of justice
- The doctrine makes forgiveness dependent on bloodshed rather than repentance and God's mercy

### 2. **Jesus Prayed Desperately NOT to Die**
- <span style="color:blue;">**Jesus:**</span> "O Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me" (Matthew 26:39) - Repeated 3 times!
- <span style="color:blue;">**Bible:**</span> His prayer was HEARD: "was heard in that he feared" (Hebrews 5:7)
- If Jesus came TO die, why pray desperately NOT to die?

### 3. **Sign of Jonah FAILED - Jesus' Own Prophecy**
- <span style="color:blue;">**Jesus:**</span> "As Jonah was THREE days and THREE nights in the whale, so shall the Son of man be THREE days and THREE nights in the heart of the earth" (Matthew 12:40)
- **Biblical timeline:**
  - Crucified: "Preparation day" = Friday afternoon (Mark 15:42, Luke 23:54, John 19:31, 42)
  - Buried: Friday before sunset (Matthew 27:57-60)
  - Resurrected: Sunday morning, "first day of the week" (Mark 16:9, Matthew 28:1)
- **Actual count:** Friday night + Saturday day + Saturday night = **1 day, 2 nights** (NOT 3 days, 3 nights)
- Jesus' own prophecy failed!

### 4. **Blood Sacrifice Contradicts the Bible**
- <span style="color:blue;">**Ezekiel 18:20:**</span> "The son shall NOT bear the iniquity of the father"
- <span style="color:blue;">**Ezekiel 18:21:**</span> "If the wicked turn from sin and keep My statutes...he shall surely live" - Repentance brings forgiveness, NO sacrifice needed
- <span style="color:blue;">**2 Chronicles 7:14:**</span> "If my people humble themselves, pray, seek my face, turn from wicked ways; I will forgive" - No blood needed
- <span style="color:blue;">**1 Samuel 15:22:**</span> "To obey is better than sacrifice"

### 5. **Jesus Taught Forgiveness WITHOUT Blood Sacrifice**
- <span style="color:blue;">**The Lord's Prayer:**</span> "Forgive us our debts as we forgive our debtors" (Matthew 6:12) - No mention of sacrifice
- <span style="color:blue;">**Zacchaeus:**</span> Repented, gave to poor, paid back what he cheated. Jesus: "TODAY salvation has come to this house" (Luke 19:9) - Salvation BEFORE crucifixion!
- <span style="color:blue;">**Nineveh:**</span> 120,000+ people forgiven through repentance and fasting alone (Jonah 3) - No blood sacrifice

### 6. **NO Eyewitness Accounts of the Crucifixion**
- <span style="color:blue;">**Matthew 26:56:**</span> "Then all the disciples forsook him, and FLED"
- <span style="color:blue;">**Mark 14:50:**</span> "And they ALL forsook him, and FLED"
- **The disciples RAN AWAY** - they were NOT eyewitnesses to the crucifixion!
- <span style="color:blue;">**Luke 23:49:**</span> "All his acquaintances stood at A DISTANCE, beholding these things"
- Even those who stayed watched from FAR AWAY - not close enough to verify who was actually crucified
- **Critical point:** If they fled and watched from a distance, how can they testify it was definitely Jesus on the cross?

# TOPIC 12: Most Misquoted Bible Verses

## üìå The Issue
> Christians often cite verses to support Trinity, Jesus' divinity, or other doctrines - but context reveals they mean something entirely different.

## ‚ö° Common Misquotations & Refutations

### 1. **"I and the Father are one" (John 10:30)**

**What Christians claim:** Proves Jesus is God, one substance with the Father.

**What it ACTUALLY means:**
- <span style="color:blue;">**John 17:21-22:**</span> Jesus prayed "that they [disciples] may be ONE, as we [Father and Son] are one...that they may be one EVEN AS WE ARE ONE"
- If "one" means same being, then ALL disciples = God too!
- Clearly means: **One in PURPOSE**, not one in essence
- <span style="color:blue;">**John 10:33-36:**</span> When Jews accused him of blasphemy, Jesus quoted Psalm 82:6 ("You are gods") - showing "son of God" is metaphorical language, not literal divinity claim

### 2. **"Before Abraham was, I am" (John 8:58)**

**What Christians claim:** "I AM" = God's name (Exodus 3:14), so Jesus claimed to be God.

**What it ACTUALLY means:**
- Greek: "eg≈ç eimi" = simply "I am" (common expression)
- Used by others without divine claim: Blind man says "eg≈ç eimi" (John 9:9) - is he God too?
- Jesus said: "Before Abraham was, I am", there is NO claim to BE God
- Many prophets pre-existed in God's knowledge (Jeremiah 1:5 - "Before I formed you in the womb I knew you")

### 3. **"In the beginning was the Word...and the Word was God" (John 1:1)**

**What Christians claim:** "Word" = Jesus, therefore Jesus is God.

**What it ACTUALLY means:**
- Greek: "kai theos ƒìn ho logos" - "and the Word was divine/godly" (NO definite article)
- Scholar's translation: "the Word was divine" (NOT "the Word was THE God")
- <span style="color:blue;">**John 1:1:**</span> "Word was WITH God" - How can Word BE God and be WITH God simultaneously?
- "Word" (Logos) = God's creative command, His wisdom - NOT a person
- <span style="color:blue;">**John 1:14:**</span> "Word became flesh" = God's command manifested in Jesus, like God's word through all prophets
- **"Logos" was adopted to match Greek philosophy:**
  - Greek philosophers (especially Philo of Alexandria and Stoics) used "Logos" to describe divine reason/intermediary between God and creation
  - John's Gospel written in Greek (90-110 CE) for Hellenistic audience - borrowed Greek philosophical concept to make Christianity appealing
  - This is Hellenization of Jesus' message - NOT what Jesus taught in Aramaic
  - Early Jewish-Christian groups (Ebionites, Nazarenes) rejected this Greek philosophical interpretation

### 4. **"My Lord and my God!" (John 20:28)**

**What Christians claim:** Thomas called Jesus "God," proving Jesus accepted worship as divine.

**What it ACTUALLY means:**
- Expression of astonishment (like "Oh my God!" today) - NOT worship
- Thomas didn't literally think Jesus was the Father (whom he called "God" separately)
- <span style="color:blue;">**John 20:17:**</span> Jesus JUST said "I am ascending to my Father and your Father, to MY GOD and your God"
- Jesus has a God = Jesus is NOT God
- If this proves divinity, why didn't Jesus confirm it? Instead he points to his own God!

### 5. **"No one comes to the Father except through me" (John 14:6)**

**What Christians claim:** Only those who believe Jesus is God/died for sins can be saved.

**What it ACTUALLY means:**
- Jesus = the way = following his MESSAGE and example
- <span style="color:blue;">**John 14:6:**</span> "I am the way, the TRUTH, and the life" - Truth and Life are metaphors; so is "the way"
- All prophets were "the way" for their people (Moses for Jews, etc.)
- Means: Follow Jesus' teaching of worshiping One God, keeping commandments
- <span style="color:blue;">**Matthew 7:21:**</span> "Not everyone who says 'Lord, Lord' will enter...but whoever DOES THE WILL of my Father"
- Salvation through OBEDIENCE to God, not just belief about Jesus

### 6. **"For God so loved the world that he gave his only begotten Son" (John 3:16)**

**What Christians claim:** Jesus is God's unique, literally begotten divine son who died for sins.

**What it ACTUALLY means:**
- "Begotten" (Greek: monogenƒìs) = unique, one-of-a-kind, NOT literally born from God
- <span style="color:blue;">**Hebrews 11:17:**</span> Isaac called Abraham's "monogenƒìs" (only begotten) - yet Abraham had Ishmael!
- Means: unique or beloved, NOT biological sonship
- Many called "son of God" in Bible: Israel (Exodus 4:22), Solomon (2 Samuel 7:14), David (Psalm 2:7), Adam (Luke 3:38)
- "Gave his son" = sent as messenger, NOT sacrifice (context: verse 17 says "not to condemn but to save")

### 7. **"I am the Alpha and Omega" (Revelation 1:8)**

**What Christians claim:** Jesus is eternal like God, proving his divinity.

**What it ACTUALLY means:**
- <span style="color:blue;">**Revelation 1:8:**</span> "I am Alpha and Omega, says the LORD GOD" - God the Father speaking, NOT Jesus
- <span style="color:blue;">**Revelation 1:1:**</span> "Revelation of Jesus Christ, which GOD GAVE UNTO HIM" - God gives revelation TO Jesus (they're separate)
- When applied to Jesus (Rev 22:13), it's in the context of being first/last messenger for that era, NOT eternal God
- <span style="color:blue;">**Isaiah 44:6:**</span> "I am the first and the last" = God alone, not shared title

**Critical Problems with This Claim:**
1. **This is a VISION/DREAM of John, NOT Jesus' actual statement**
   - Revelation 22:13 occurs in John's vision - not Jesus walking around making this claim
   - Christians demand statements FROM Jesus himself, but this is John's dream/vision
   - Visions and dreams are not the same authority as direct statements

2. **"Alpha and Omega" = Eternality, but eternality alone does NOT prove divinity**
   - <span style="color:blue;">**Melchizedek (Hebrews 7:1-4):**</span> "Without father, without mother, without genealogy, having neither beginning of days nor end of life"
   - Melchizedek described as having NO beginning and NO end (eternal attributes)
   - Yet Melchizedek is NOT God and is NOT Jesus
   - If eternality proved divinity, Melchizedek would be God too!

3. **"First and Last" is about role/position, not eternality**
   - Could mean first and last messenger for a specific era or mission
   - Does not necessarily mean eternal existence
   - Context matters: Is this about temporal priority or ontological nature?

4. **Major contradiction: How can Jesus be "Alpha and Omega" if he has a beginning?**
   - <span style="color:blue;">**Revelation 3:14:**</span> Jesus is "the beginning of the creation of God"
   - <span style="color:blue;">**Colossians 1:15:**</span> Jesus is "the firstborn of all creation"
   - If Jesus is created/born, he has a beginning - contradicts being "Alpha" (no beginning)!
